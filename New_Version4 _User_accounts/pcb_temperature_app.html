The backend now assembles the steady-state finite-difference equations into a sparse matrix and solves A T = b directly. The legacy SOR omega value is kept here only for comparison or future fallback iterative runs; the sparse direct solve does not use omega.

dx, dy, and dz define the 3D voxel size. Smaller values resolve copper layers and component heat spreading better, but increase the sparse matrix size and solve time. dz is especially important for thin copper layers.
